Bubble Tea Encyclopedia

Discover the history of bubble tea, types of bubble tea, and common bubble tea flavors and toppings.

The History of Bubble Tea

Bubble Tea originated in small tea stands in Taiwan in the early 1980s. In the 1980s, Bubble Tea was just a drink with some sort of tea and powdered flavors. But, in 1983 Liu Han-Chieh introduced tapioca pearls which later became the most popular addition to bubble tea drinks. Today, there are a bunch of bubble tea shops, like Kung Fu Tea. To learn more about bubble tea go to bubbleteasupply.com.

Fun Fact: Bubble Tea is also known as Boba Drink, Pearl Drink, Tapioca Ball Drink, and QQ.

Fun Fact: Bubble Tea is usually served in clear cups, to see the add ons, and with fat straws, to make the tapioca pearls accessible.

Fun Fact: Tapioca Pearls can be black, white, or transparent, and they are often described as chewy or jello-y.

Types of Bubble Tea

Table of Types of Bubble Tea
Name Description
Classic Tea Tea is the only ingredient added to the drink for flavor.
Milk Tea Tea that is infused with milk. It may have include additional flavors.
Punch/Fruit Tea that either taste like fruit and/or contain bits of fruit.
Slush/Smoothie Smoothies with tapioca pearls.
Yogurt Tea that contains yogurt.
